A Note on Honesty

Honesty is so often cited as a value that it almost becomes cliche. The combination of Courage and Integrity applied to honesty covers its positive aspects well. Be brave enough to share your thoughts and feelings, but also be wise enough to keep some words to yourself. Be wary of pathological transparency. The purpose of honesty is for your Integrity, to be a true version of yourself. Do that and life usually becomes less complicated and more enjoyable.

Virtues

Marcus Aurelius Virtues

Timeless wisdom from Stoic philosophy applicable to modern life and work.

  • • Wisdom: The pursuit of knowledge and understanding
  • • Justice: Treating others fairly and with respect
  • • Courage: Facing challenges with determination
  • • Temperance: Practicing moderation and self-control

Virtues of a Programmer

Larry Wall's three great virtues that make a great programmer.

  • • Laziness: The quality that drives you to reduce overall effort
  • • Impatience: The anger you feel when the computer is being lazy
  • • Hubris: Excessive pride that makes you write maintainable programs
